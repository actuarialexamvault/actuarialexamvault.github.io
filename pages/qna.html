Questions & Answers

Frequently asked questions about using Actuarial Exam Vault

Getting Started

How do I start practicing with past papers?

Navigate to the Subjects page, select your subject, then choose a year and paper. Click "Start Exam" to begin a timed practice session.

What subjects are available?

We currently support all ASSA administered subjects including A111, A211, A311, N211, F101-F108 (exlc. F106), and F201-F204. Past papers are available from 2018 onwards where available from the ASSA website.

Do I need to download anything?

No downloads required! Actuarial Exam Vault is a web-based platform. Simply create an account and start practicing from any device with internet access.

Exam Practice

How do I upload my exam answers?

After completing a practice exam, click "Upload Submission" and select your answer file (PDF, Word, or image format). Your file is stored locally on your device for privacy. It will not be shared with anyone including the developer.

Can I pause an exam once started?

No, the timer cannot be paused once you've started an exam. However, you can cancel your current attempt and restart at a later time. This design helps replicate authentic exam conditions for better practice.

What file formats are supported for submissions?

We support PDF (.pdf) and Word documents (.doc, .docx). Files up to 50MB are accepted.

Can I download my previous exam attempts?

Yes, you can download any of your previous submissions from the exam review page. However, files can only be downloaded from the same device where they were originally uploaded, as your answer files are stored locally on your device for privacy. While the files remain on your device, metadata about your attempts (exam details, grades, timestamps) is stored in the cloud and syncs across all your devices.

Grading & Review

How does the grading system work?

You can grade your own work question by question using the examiner's report as guidance. Enter marks for each question, and the system calculates your overall score automatically.

Is AI grading available?

Yes! AI-assisted grading is available using your own preferred AI chatbot (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.). When reviewing a question, select "AI-Review" and copy our specially engineered grading prompt for consistent, realistic marking aligned with professional actuarial standards.

Can I edit my grades after saving?

Yes, you can revisit any graded exam and update question grades at any time. Changes are saved automatically and reflected in your progress statistics.

Where can I find examiner's reports?

Examiner's reports are accessible alongside exam papers when available. Look for the "Examiner's Report" link on the paper selection page.

AI-Assisted Grading

How does AI-assisted grading work?

Our platform provides a carefully engineered grading prompt that you can use with any AI chatbot (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.). Simply copy the prompt, paste it into your chosen AI tool along with the question, examiner's report, and your answer, then input the AI's score into our system.

How was the AI grading prompt designed?

Our AI grading prompt was engineered to replicate how real actuarial examiners mark papers. It emphasizes strict adherence to marking standards, proper point allocation (0.5 marks per valid point), assessment of key competencies (idea identification, information use, conciseness, idea generation), and focuses on demonstrating professional actuarial competence rather than lenient marking.

Can I customize the AI grading prompt?

Yes, you can adjust the prompt to your preferences. However, ensure you maintain consistency across all your grading sessions. If you modify the prompt, use the same version for all questions to ensure fair comparison of your performance over time.

What are the limitations of AI grading?

AI can sometimes hallucinate or make errors. Its marking quality can degrade over longer conversations. Always review the AI's feedback critically and compare it against the official examiner's report. If something seems off, trust the examiner's report or seek clarification from study groups or tutors.

Should I grade multiple questions in one AI chat session?

No, we strongly recommend grading only one question per AI chat session. Start a fresh conversation for each question to maintain consistent marking quality and avoid context degradation that can occur in longer chat windows.

Best practices for AI-assisted grading?

Key tips:
• Grade one question per chat session for consistency
• Always provide the examiner's report alongside your answer
• Include the question text and maximum marks available
• Review AI feedback critically - check for hallucinations
• Use the same AI model consistently for comparable results
• Cross-reference with official examiner's reports when uncertain
• Keep the AI grading prompt unchanged for consistency
• Document any prompt modifications you make for future reference

Which AI chatbot should I use for grading?

Popular options include ChatGPT (GPT-4 or GPT-4o for best results), Claude (Anthropic), and Gemini (Google). Choose one and stick with it for consistency. Paid versions generally provide more detailed and accurate feedback than free tiers.

How do I structure my AI grading request?

Include: (1) Our provided grading prompt, (2) The question text and marks available, (3) The relevant section from the examiner's report, (4) Your complete answer. Ask the AI to provide both a numerical score and qualitative feedback on your performance.

Progress Tracking

How is my progress calculated?

Your progress shows the number of papers attempted out of total available papers with PDFs. The performance graph displays your average score for each graded paper, sorted by completion date.

Can I track progress across multiple subjects?

Absolutely! The Progress Tracker page lets you view statistics for each subject separately, including attempted papers, grades, and performance trends.

What does "Not graded" mean?

This indicates you've completed and uploaded an exam but haven't graded it yet. Visit the grading page to review and score your submission.

Technical & Account

Is my data secure?

Yes! We use Firebase Authentication for secure login. Your uploaded exam files are stored locally on your device, and only metadata (exam info, grades, timestamps) is synced to the cloud.

Can I access my data from multiple devices?

Your account data (exam attempts, grades, progress) syncs across devices. However, uploaded files are stored locally per device for privacy and performance.

What happens if I forget my password?

Click "Forgot Password" on the sign-in page to receive a password reset email. Follow the link to create a new password and regain access to your account.

The page is loading slowly. What should I do?

Try refreshing the page or clearing your browser cache. If issues persist, check your internet connection or try a different browser. Contact support if problems continue.

Can I delete my account?

Yes, contact us at actuarial-exam-vault@gmail.com to request account deletion. We'll remove your data within 5 business days.

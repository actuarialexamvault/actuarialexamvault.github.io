## QUESTION 3

A life insurance company has, for a number of years, sold, on varying terms, a single premium unitlinked endowment assurance product that has a guaranteed maturity value of R1 million. Based on the company's internal calculations the maturity value of such a policy (ignoring the guarantee) is estimated to have a normal distribution with mean of R1.3 million and standard deviation of R0.2 million. Assume that policies cannot be surrendered before maturity.
i. State the factors that would influence the statistical distribution of the maturity value.
[4]

ii. Calculate the probability that the maturity guarantee for such a policy will bite.
[3]

iii. Assuming that the underlying assets are invested passively in a fund matching a main local
index, explain how the company can hedge its risk exposure arising from this guarantee.

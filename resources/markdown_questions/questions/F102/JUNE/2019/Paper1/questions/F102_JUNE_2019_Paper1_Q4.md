## QUESTION 4

A life insurance company has, for a number of years, sold a single premium unit-linked endowment policy with the following features:

- There is no death benefit.

- The maturity benefit is equal to the value of units at maturity, subject to a guaranteed minimum amount equal to the single premium.

- The surrender value is equal to the value of units, less a surrender penalty.

- The single premium is invested in a venture capital fund.

- A percentage of the premium is allocated to the unit fund at policy inception.

- Management charges are deducted from the fund on a monthly basis.

i. Describe two different methods that the insurer could use to value the cost of the guaranteed
minimum maturity value.
[4]

Over the past few years, equity markets have shown higher volatility than in previous years. There has been a recent fall in equity market values of 30%, including venture capital equity investments.
ii. Explain how the recent investment market experience would have impacted on the relative
position of the venture capital investment product's assets and liabilities.

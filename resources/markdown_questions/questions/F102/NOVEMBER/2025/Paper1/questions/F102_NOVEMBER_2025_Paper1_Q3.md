## QUESTION 3

A life insurance company sells a regular premium unit-linked endowment assurance policy with the following features:

- The benefit payable on maturity is the value of the unit fund (if positive).

- The benefit payable on death is the value of the unit fund (if positive), subject to a minimum guaranteed sum assured specified at inception by the policyholder.

- The surrender value is equal to the bid value of units less a surrender penalty (if positive).

- The policyholder may select from several local and overseas investment portfolios.

- The only product charges are a risk charge and fund management charge, both of which are deducted monthly from the unit fund.

- The policyholder can specify the monthly premium at inception, but it is subject to a minimum premium specific to each policy.

i. Outline briefly the main client needs that are met by this product.
[3]

ii. Explain briefly why the insurance company would specify minimum premiums.
[2]

iii. Outline briefly how the insurer would calculate an expropriation price of a unit.
[3]

iv. Describe 2 examples which demonstrate how the calculation of unit prices may affect equity
between different generations of policyholders. Assume there are no calculation errors.
[3]
